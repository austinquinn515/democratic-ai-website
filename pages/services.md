## How Engagements Are Structured

The service model is built around three tiers, designed to meet professionals at whatever stage of readiness they are actually at, not an idealized version of where a consultant would like them to be.

Every tier starts from the same place: understanding the human first. The infrastructure comes after.

---

## Tier 1 — Diagnostic Engagements

For professionals who are early in their thinking, want to understand what AI can genuinely do for their practice before committing to implementation, or need a clear picture to make the case internally.

These are short, focused engagements that deliver a specific, actionable output. No generic recommendations. No 40-page reports that sit on a shelf.

**AI Readiness Assessment**
A two-week engagement that evaluates your current workflow, identifies the specific points where AI can create genuine leverage, and produces a written priority roadmap for implementation. Designed for professionals who want clarity before they commit.
$1,500 — $2,500

**Voice Profile Document**
A one-week engagement that produces a custom voice and brand guide built specifically around the way you communicate. The foundation for any AI content system that is going to sound like you rather than like everyone else using the same tools.
$1,000 — $1,800

**Workflow Audit**
A two-week engagement that maps your current practice workflow, identifies the highest-leverage points for AI intervention, and delivers a prioritized recommendation set with clear rationale for each.
$1,500 — $2,500

---

## Tier 2 — Implementation Engagements

The core offering. Four to six week engagements that move from diagnosis through to a functioning AI system you can operate independently. Every implementation is built from scratch around your specific practice, your specific voice, and your specific clients.

**AI Practice Infrastructure Build — Financial Services**
For wealth managers, RIAs, and independent advisory firms. A custom AI system for client communication that matches your firm's voice, a research workflow that compresses prep time without sacrificing depth, and a compliance-aware architecture that knows where automation is appropriate and where human review is non-negotiable.
$3,500 — $6,000

**Voice Infrastructure Build — Content Creators and Professionals**
For consultants, attorneys, coaches, and newsletter operators who need consistent content volume without content that sounds nothing like them. A complete prompt architecture built around your writing patterns, a practical production workflow, and a 30-day refinement period after delivery.
$2,500 — $4,500

**Behavioral Adoption Program — Teams and Practices**
For firms where the challenge is not figuring out what AI tools to use but getting a team to actually use them well. A psychology-informed adoption program that addresses the specific friction points that cause AI implementation to stall, delivered with a phased rollout designed around how people actually change rather than how they are supposed to change.
$3,000 — $5,500

---

## Tier 3 — Retainer and Ongoing Support

For clients who have completed an implementation engagement and want continued access to the founder's judgment as their practice and the AI landscape evolve.

**Monthly Retainer**
Four to six hours of ongoing support per month, covering system refinement, new tool evaluation, and any adjustments as your workflow changes.
$1,200 — $2,000 per month

**Quarterly Review**
One focused session reviewing system performance, updating voice documentation as needed, and identifying any new opportunities in the practice.
$800 — $1,200 per quarter

---

## A Note on Pricing

All pricing reflects founder-led delivery. There is no junior consultant on these engagements. The range within each tier reflects scope variation, not tiered access to quality. Every client at every price point works directly with Austin.

If you are not sure which tier fits your situation, the AI Readiness Assessment is the natural starting point. It is designed to give you enough clarity to know whether a larger implementation engagement is the right next step.
