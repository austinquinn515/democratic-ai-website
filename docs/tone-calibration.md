# Tone Calibration Notes

*For anyone writing or editing copy for Democratic AI Consulting*

---

## The Brand Signal

The copy across all pages is deliberately understated. The brand signals through **restraint, specificity, and precision** — not energy or enthusiasm.

A prospect who resonates with this positioning will be drawn in by how seriously the language takes the subject. They are already tired of AI hype. They are looking for someone who has something real to say.

---

## What to Avoid

- Exclamation points (never)
- Intensifiers: "incredibly," "powerful," "game-changing," "revolutionary"
- Manufactured urgency in CTAs ("Act now," "Limited time")
- Generic AI buzzwords: "cutting-edge," "innovative," "leverage the power of"
- Passive constructions that distance the founder from the work
- Hedges that undermine authority: "I think," "perhaps," "sort of"

---

## What to Preserve

- First-person specificity when the founder is speaking
- Present-tense declaratives: "The goal is..." not "Our goal would be..."
- Short sentences after long ones — rhythm matters
- Em dashes for parenthetical precision (used sparingly)
- Prices stated plainly, not buried

---

## Editing Rule

> If any page copy needs to be shortened for a specific Squarespace section, prioritize removing context before removing specificity. A shorter sentence that is still precise beats a longer sentence that is still vague.

---

## Voice Calibration Test

Read the copy out loud. If it sounds like a consultant explaining something they genuinely understand to someone they respect, it is right. If it sounds like a pitch deck, revise it.
